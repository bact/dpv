Human Subject

<ghurlbot> Issue 205 [Concept]: `HumanSubject` as the parent of `DataSubject` and `AISubject` (by coolharsh55)

harsh: we discussed previously that our existing DataSubject taxonomy is a good resource and would be helpful to have it being used also alongside other Subject concepts such as AISubject. For this, we proposed adding HumanSubject as the top concept under which the categories will be present, and then DataSubject etc. will be subclasses of this concept. Breaks backwards compatibility potentially in a minor but easily fixable way, but the benefits are greater in favour of doing this.

iainHenderson: +1 to do this

georgKrog: What about Organisations that are Customers - does this concept also cover that?

harsh: no, it doesn't - we specifically model only natural persons or humans and modelling organisational interrelationships is outside of our scope for now. In the future, if we want to do this, it will have to be a separate extension just for organisations and non-humans.

georgKrog: +1 for now as having these categories for humans would be helpful

paulRyan: What will happen to DataSubject being the top concept?

harsh: It will be under HumanSubject so you can say a human category like Tourist is a data subject and it will be okay / correct - no compatibility issues with existing uses like this.

paulRyan: +1 to go ahead with the approach

delaramGolpayegani: how will this work with AISubject?

harsh: we will have HumanSubject, and then subclasses DataSubject in DPV, TechnologySubject in TECH and its subclass AISubject in AI extension

delaramGolpayegani: What about other non-human subjects such as animals which might be what AI is used on?

harsh: outside of our scope for now - same as the issue with organisations as subjects. In the future we can think about this, but then we will try to explicitly denote they are non-human subjects

georgKrog: how will this work with natural persons and legal persons?

harsh: HumanSubject will be a specific kind of NaturalPerson, and all natural persons are legal persons (we have LegalEntity as the concept for this)

discussion concluded with agreement on adding this concept

ACTION: Add Human Subject

Tracking

<ghurlbot> Issue 206 [Concept]: `Tracking` (by coolharsh55)

harsh: as discussed previously, P7012 implementation will require concepts that are not currently present in DPV; some such as ServiceData aren't clearly defined so we can put them in the extension, but others such as Tracking are already used and have a technical definition (e.g. see Do-Not-Track). Therefore we proposed to have this concept within DPV alongside the existing Profiling concept as a Processing operation that extends Use category of processing. This is because tracking uses data, and in that process it may also collect and generate additional data. But the minimal definition is that it uses some data to track the person across contexts.

georgKrog: DSA has profiling, so maybe we should wait for more info on that?

harsh: we already have Profiling in DPV, and in addition to this, we have profiling in the GDPR extension as defined in Art.4-4, and we can always update the definitions and concepts as the law and regulatory context evolves

georgKrog: DSA defines profiling as it is defined in GDPR Art.4-4 as well - so anything that supports expressing these would be good

harsh: so then we should add it - should we also add First Party Tracking and Third Party Tracking as these are also required for P7012 and are commonly used?

iainHenderson: yes, these would be good to have

georgKrog: how would these be defined?

harsh: We will base these on existing definitions for the moment - though first and third party are not clearly defined concepts in legal terms for us to rely on them

Discussion concluded with agreement on adding tracking and 1P, 3P variations with a note that it may evolve in the future

ACTION: Add Tracking concepts, with 1P and 3P variations

Technology Provision

<ghurlbot> Issue 207 [Concept]: Change `TechnologyProvisionMethod` concepts e.g. `tech:System` to `tech:ProvidedAsSystem` (by coolharsh55)

harsh: in previous discussion, we went through how the existing tech provision methods are confusing as they contain concepts like System while the AI extension has AISystem - which are two distinct interpretations. To avoid this, the proposal is to have the TECH extension use System, Service, etc. as types of technologies, and to create distinct concepts representing how they are provided, and to add a prefix or suffix to the concept to denote this.

georgKrog: for consent via privacy notice, where you provide the notice through a method, and then you collect it through a method - what is being described here?

harsh: in this case, the notice or dialogue is a technology, and we are describing how it is provided e.g. on a website it is a component as it is operating within the website (as a system), similarly other technologies might be subscriptions or entire systems (independent)

harsh: Some options for the terms: 1) ProvidedAsSystem - we add prefixed Provided as to denote this is a type of provision; 2) SystemProvision - we add suffix Provision to denote it is being provisioned; 3) SystemProvisionMethod - we add suffix Provision Method to denote we are describing the method of provision. My preference is for n.1 as it is most clear and simple

georgKrog: +1 for n.1

julianFlake: +1 for n.1

ACTION: Change TechnologyProvisionMethod concepts to have prefix ProvidedAsX

SECTOR

<ghurlbot> Issue 204 Create Sector Extension (by coolharsh55)

harsh: as discussed last week, we have lots of concepts in the SECTOR extension - at the moment these are only purposes but we will also expect personal data and other things in the future - as Beatriz pointed out we may want to think about further modularising the concepts. So the question is whether we have one large extension and then the HTML has different sections for each sector or do we have modular smaller extensions within sector for each specific sector like what we do for the legal extensions? (see example on github in sector branch)

georgKrog: within each sector there will be domains that are the same, so maybe having domains is an alternative?

harsh: what do you mean by sector and domain here?

georgKrog: e.g. the HR in Health and Finance being two different things

harsh: we can do it this way - for general domains that are expected to be widely present, we keep them in DPV as we already have them in DPV e.g. the job hiring and other HR stuff; then the sector specific concepts or the sector-specific interpretation of domain concepts can go in the sector extensions e.g. employee in Health could be doctors, and employee evaluation for doctors means a very specific thing - so that can be extended in the sector extension

georgKrog: yes, modular is better - so let's go with that approach

Discussion concluded with agreement on using modular approach for sector

ACTION: Use modular approach in SECTOR extension for each sector
